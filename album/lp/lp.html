LP!

JPEGMAFIA 57 mins
Released Nov 2021 · Republic (reluctantly)
8.6

Review

LP! represents JPEGMAFIA at his most technically refined and creatively liberated—his fourth studio album and final release under Republic Records is a sprawling statement of artistic independence that somehow manages to be his most accessible and experimental work simultaneously. The offline version especially showcases Peggy's complete vision: glitchy, video-gamey production that blurs genre lines through unorthodox sampling, from flipping old R&B tunes into ethereal beasts to layering Evangelion-like synths, all while he produces, writes, mixes, and masters everything himself. What makes it his best is the sheer maturation of his sound—the production quality has evolved with each record, and here he's perfected the balance between the abrasive noise that made him notorious and the melodic sensibilities that make tracks like his Britney Spears interpolation both confrontational and oddly beautiful. It's a refreshing drink of water in the burning heat of unoriginality, with Peggy piercing through experimental production frontiers while maintaining the satirical edge that tackles internet culture, industry exploitation, and his own artistic frustrations with a controlled anger that never sacrifices his playful idiosyncrasies. The album's dual existence—censored streaming version versus his true offline vision—is itself a middle finger to label constraints, making LP! not just his technically strongest work but his most conceptually complete artistic statement.
